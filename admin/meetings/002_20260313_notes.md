### Project and Ideas
#### The Crux
Professor will create and "post" questions to the class to answer. The primary goal of the project is to grant the ability to the professor to collect drawings and draw a random subset (base goal).
- A starting idea is to have professor start with a subset of student responses akin to post-it notes that he can categorize/sort them on the screen. Alternatively/additionally, have some sort of quick-reject-or-accept system for professor to quickly sift through responses.

Other notes:
- Nice-to-haves/future things to think about:
  - Allow TAs to view student responses coming in too.
  - Expect about 200-300 simutaneous connections (what are our system constraints?)
- Cautions:
  - While `@ucsd.edu` email auth. is good, this can pose an issue with international students who may not have such an email (Professor has mentioned he typically would expect 2-3 cases per quarter). 
- Professor's considerations:
  - **A class survey can be conducted to gather information about student devices and availability** (e.g. browsers, powerful/weak devices, device preferences, etc). This can be done at any point during the quarter.
  - Make the project public so that (1) in case this goes south, someone else could continue it if they wished to (2) this project can be used for classes that are not necessarily COGS 108.
- It may be a good idea to research what similar apps/software exist currently to get ideas/inspiration. Professor mentioned that he hasn't seen one yet that provides the drawing aspect he's looking for.
